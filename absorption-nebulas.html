ABSORPTION NEBULAS

What is an Absorption Nebula?

Also known as dark nebulas, these nebulas are so dense that they actually block the light behind them. It's kind of like when a storm cloud has so much water in it that it blots out the sun and makes the sky look dark gray. The reason we are able to see these nebulas at all is thanks to an emission nebula behind it. As mentioned earlier, a nebula can be more than one type at a time, and in the case of the absorption nebula, we only know it's there because it blocks out the light of whatever is behind it (whether that be an emission nebula or just a star cluster). Many absorption nebulas just look like a hole in space (see below for examples).


Why do we call it an Absorption Nebula?

These clouds of dust absorb any light behind them, rather than reflecting it. This is because they're so dense that they just block the light behind them, meaning that, from Earth, we only see the outline of the cloud.
