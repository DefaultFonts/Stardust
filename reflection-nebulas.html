REFLECTION NEBULAS

What is a Reflection Nebula?

Unlike an emission nebula, this type of nebula can only be seen thanks to the light of nearby stars. These clouds are often a lot wispier. A reflection nebula will always be backlit â€“ if it wasn't, it wouldn't be considered a reflection nebula, because we wouldn't be able to see it. Although there may be a cloud of dust and gas there, we can't see it unless a star is able to light it up. Because they get their light from the stars behind them, we can't actually see the true colour of the gas and dust. The cloud will actually usually appear blue since it reflects that colour of light more easily. It's the same reason our sky is blue.


Why do we call it a Reflection Nebula?

Like most (non-star) objects in space, the dust found in a reflection nebula can only be seen by reflecting the light of a nearby star. It doesn't produce its own light, it just reflects the light around it.
