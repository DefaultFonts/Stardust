PLANETARY NEBULAS

What is a Planetary Nebula?

A type of emission nebula produced by the death of a low-mass star. When a star runs out of hydrogen to fuse, it will eject its outermost layer, and then continue fusion with helium. It follows this pattern until it runs out of fusion material, at which point it fully dies. This process is responsible for the concentric circular patterns that can sometimes be found in planetary nebulas. Additionally, because the star continues fusion after ejecting its outer layer, that dust and gas become hot enough to glow â€“ making it an emission nebula. When our sun eventually dies, it will become this type of nebula.


Why do we call it a Planetary Nebula?

This name is the least logical of the bunch. When planetary nebulas were first discovered, astronomers were only able to see them through a small telescope and thought they kind of looked like planets. It's worth mentioning that they actually don't have anything to do with planets.
